Autowagen Master — Accounts receivable report & customer statement

Plain-English walkthrough of each area on the two screens (matches Reports → Accounts receivable and the printable statement). Save as PDF: Ctrl+P → Save as PDF. File: docs/ar_report_and_customer_statement_explained_print.html

Open in browser: from your project folder, double-click this file — or from the app use docs/client_training_index.html (link added there).

A. “Customer receivables” report (customer_ar_report.php)

You open this from the black top menu: Reports → Accounts receivable (owed). It answers: which customers still owe us money on final sales invoices? It does not list money you owe suppliers (that is Reports → Accounts payable).

A1 — Grey box: “Credit notes — locked rules”

This is a short reminder of how credit notes affect the numbers (after sql/07_credit_notes.sql is applied):

  • The Balance column is net due: what the customer still owes on that invoice after normal payments and after all finalized credits (both types below).
  • Formula (per invoice): Invoice total (incl. VAT) − Sum(payments) − Sum(finalized credit notes on that invoice)
  • AR cr. and Refund (or Refund cn. on some screens) only split the credit-note total into two buckets so your books can tell a story:
AR reduction credits add to the AR cr. column total.
Cash refund credits add to the Refund column total.
Both types still reduce Balance the same way — the split does not invent a second, different balance.

If you see R 0.00 in AR cr. and Refund, that usually means no finalized credit notes exist for those invoices yet — not that the feature is broken.

A2 — Grey box: “Two different reports”

This stops the common confusion between:

  • This page: money customers owe you (sales / POS).
  • Accounts payable: money you owe suppliers (inventory purchases). Different menu, different data.

A3 — Grey line: “Overdue” definition

Overdue here means: the invoice has a meaningful due date, that date is before the report’s As at date, and there is still a positive balance. Invoices without a due date still show in totals and balances, but they are not flagged as overdue on this logic.

A4 — Filter row: date, ticks, Apply, buttons

  • As at — calendar date (“pretend today is …”). Due dates are compared to this when deciding overdue portions.
  • Overdue only — narrows lists to balances that qualify as overdue as at that date.
  • Account customers only — only customers flagged as account customers on their record (requires Stage 6a SQL). Useful when you mainly sell “on account” to a subset.
  • Apply — reloads the report with your filters.
  • Print / PDF — hides navigation for a cleaner print or saved PDF.
  • Sales invoices — shortcut to the invoice list (invoices_admin.php), not AR math.

A5 — Summary strip (black bar + red side card)

  • Total owed (listed customers) — sum of positive net due balances for everyone who appeared in the By customer table under your filters.
  • Of which overdue — sum of balance amounts that fall on overdue invoices only (still using the rules in A3).

A6 — Section: “BY CUSTOMER”

One row per customer who has outstanding balance (under your filters). Columns:

  • Customer — name on file.
  • Account — shows an “Account” badge if that customer is an account customer.
  • Phone — contact if stored.
  • Balance — that customer’s total net due across all their open final invoices.
  • AR cr. — total of finalized AR reduction credit-note amounts attached to their invoices (classification only).
  • Refund — total of finalized cash refund credits on those invoices.
  • Overdue — portion of their balance counted as overdue (see A3).
  • Statement / Customer — open printable statement or customer edit.

A7 — Section: “BY INVOICE (open balance)”

One row per final invoice that still has money owed (positive net due). Columns:

  • Invoice — e.g. INV-2026-00002.
  • Customer — buyer.
  • Inv date — invoice date.
  • Due date — for on-account; “—” or empty if none set.
  • Status — badge like Overdue Nd, Open, No due date — quick read of timing.
  • Balance — net due on this invoice alone.
  • AR cr. — sum of finalized AR-reduction credits for this invoice; shows “—” or R 0.00 when none.
  • Refund cn. — sum of finalized cash-refund credits; “—” or R 0.00 when none.
  • Open — jump to invoice in POS (invoice_edit.php).

B. Customer account statement (customer_statement.php)

This is a printable “letter-style” overview for one customer. Typical ways to open it: next to their name on the Customers list, link from AR report Statement, or direct URL ?id= with their customer ID.

B1 — Title block

  • Statement date: the “as at” calendar date printed on the statement (Johannesburg). May differ slightly from invoice dates inside the grid.
  • Currency ZAR, VAT as per invoices — amounts follow what was finalized on each invoice.

B2 — “Bill to” card

Name, optional billing address, phone, email, and optionally an “Account customer” badge — exactly what will print on PDF for the buyer.

B3 — “Amount outstanding” (dark box)

Single headline number: sum of Balance on all listed invoice rows below that still have something due (normally excludes invoices fully paid/zero balance unless you chose to include settled ones).

B4 — Toolbar: date, checkbox, Apply, buttons

  • Statement as at — adjusts which overdue styling might apply vs that date.
  • Include paid-in-full — when ticked and applied, invoices with R 0.00 balance can appear too (history).
  • Print / PDF — save a formal attachment.
  • WhatsApp — opens WhatsApp Web/app with a short text summary (not full letterhead).
  • Email — mailto:; opens Outlook/Thunderbird etc. with pasted summary text — still not automatic server email.
  • Edit customer / AR report — shortcuts.

B5 — Grey explanatory box (credit notes + sending)

Repeats in customer language:

Balance = total − paid − all finalized credits.
AR cr. and Refund cn. show how much credit was labelled each way.
Full line-level detail stays on each credit note and invoice.

Also warns that SMTP (automatic email from the server) is not wired yet — Email button is desktop mail.

B6 — Invoices table

  • Invoice / Date / Due — identity and timing.
  • Total — invoice total incl. VAT.
  • Paid — payments recorded against that invoice.
  • AR cr. — finalized AR-reduction credits for that invoice (R 0.00 when none).
  • Refund cn. — finalized cash-refund credits for that invoice (R 0.00 when none).
  • Balance — remaining customer obligation on that invoice = same net rule as the AR report row for that INV.
  • Open — drill into invoice screen.

Foot row Total outstanding should match or align with the dark summary box when filters match.

B7 — “Recent payments received” (if shown)

List of payment lines tied to invoices for this customer (most recent first, capped). Good for callers asking “did you allocate my EFT?” — paired with invoices above.

Why both screens?
The AR report is operational: whom to chase, overdue focus, totals across everyone.
The statement is relational: send to one customer showing their invoices + split credit columns without logging into back-office jargon.
