Supplier purchase screen — all sections explained

Autowagen Master · For shop staff and training · Example URL pattern: supplier_purchase_edit.php?id=2 (Purchase #2)

Save as PDF: Open this file in Chrome or Edge → Ctrl+P → Save as PDF / Microsoft Print to PDF → Save.
How to open this screen in the app: Sign in → top menu Inventory → Supplier purchases → click a purchase row, or use New supplier purchase to create one. You can also open a purchase from All parts via the Purchase # link in the From column.

Screen map (top to bottom)

Letters A–G match the teaching layout. The real page may show a small All purchases link next to the title.

TOP

Page title and short intro

What: The heading (e.g. Purchase #2) and one or two lines of help text: choose who you bought from, then add parts to this purchase.

Why: One purchase = one seller (or one batch deal) and many part lines. The intro reminds staff of that rule.

When: Every time you open the page — read it once when training; no button to click here.

A

Reference

What: Invoice / bundle name (optional) and Notes (free text).

Why: So you can find this buy later (e.g. “inv002”, delivery instructions, internal reminders).

When: When you create the purchase or change the reference or notes.

How: Edit the fields → click red Save purchase (section E).

B

Bill (ZAR) — what you owe for this buy

What: Bill / invoice amount, bill date, optional due date.

Why: This is the money you owe the supplier for this batch. It feeds Remaining in Payments (F) and the report Reports → Accounts payable (owed). It is not the sum of part “asking” prices — those are your sell prices per line (G).

When: When you know the supplier’s total (you can update if the invoice was wrong).

How: Fill in → Save purchase (E).

C

Supplier or private seller

What: Toggle: Registered supplier (dropdown) or Private individual (name, SA ID, phone).

Why: Clear record of who you bought from. One seller per purchase; every part added under this purchase belongs to that deal.

When: At setup; again only if seller details change.

How: Select supplier or fill private fields → Save purchase (E).

D

ID / address on file

What: File uploads: ID / CIPC scan, Proof of residence, and the “I have this on file” tick where it applies.

Why: Second-Hand Goods Act style paper trail, especially for private sellers. A registered company supplier may often leave these empty.

When: When you receive scans or replace documents.

How: Choose file → Save purchase (E).

E

Save purchase (red button)

What: Submits the main form: Reference (A), Bill (B), Supplier/private (C), uploads (D).

Why: Payments use a separate small form and the blue Add button — so the red button only saves the “header” and files.

When: After any change to A, B, C, or D.

Not for: Logging payments — use F → Add.

F

Payments (ZAR)

What: Summary: Remaining, Bill, Paid; list of past payments; form: amount, paid-on date, method, bank ref, note, then Add.

Why: The bill is what you owe; each payment line is money you already sent. Remaining = bill minus total paid (for this purchase).

When: Each time you pay this supplier for this purchase (full or part payment — part payment = multiple lines).

How: Fill the row → blue Add. Do not use Save purchase for this.

G

Parts in this purchase

What: Table: SKU, part name, line type (e.g. OEM new, replacement), asking price, Edit; button Add part to this purchase.

Why: Links physical stock lines to this buy. Seller and compliance stay on the purchase; each part holds selling price and other part details (yard, etc.) on the part edit screen.

When: When stock arrives or when you need to fix a line.

How: Add part… or Edit — save on those pages. Changing only the part does not require Save purchase unless you also changed A–D.

APP

App footer (bottom of every page)

What: Copyright line, version (e.g. v1.0), sometimes a DEBUG badge in development.

Why: Standard app information — not part of purchase workflow.

When: No action required.

One-line rules for staff

  • Header (reference, bill, seller, files) → Save purchase.
  • Money out to supplier for this purchase → Payments → Add.
  • Stock lines → Parts in this purchase → Add part / Edit part screens.
