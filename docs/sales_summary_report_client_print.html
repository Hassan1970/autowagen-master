AUTOWAGEN MASTER

Client handout — Sales summary (period) report · what it shows · how to run it · sample screen (illustration only). Suitable for accountants, silent partners, or staff training.
To make your PDF: Press Ctrl+P → choose Save as PDF or Microsoft Print to PDF → turn Background graphics On so the heading bar stays black/red.

1 · What this report is for

The Sales summary helps you answer, for any date range:

  • How much did we sell over the counter / POS? (totals from finalised invoices dated in your range).
  • How much cash / EFT / card did we actually record as received in that same calendar period?(sum of payment lines by their paid date — sometimes different from the sale date).
  • Who were our busiest customers? (ranked list for final sales in that range).
  • Staff vs labour lines: how many invoice lines linked to physical parts vs free-text manual lines (labour/fees).
Important limits. This report uses workshop POS invoices only. Guest orders from your public website (WEB‑… orders) are managed under Reports → Web shop orders, not on this screen. Customer returns / proper credit notes as formal documents are not in the app yet — use your normal bookkeeping process until that module exists.

2 · The two headline numbers clients ask about

IdeaWhat the report countsUse when …
Sales turnover Each final invoice total (incl. VAT) whose invoice date falls inside From → To. You want turnover for a month/quarter aligned to invoice date (traditional “what we invoiced”).
Payments in Payment rows logged on invoices, summed by each row’s paid date inside the range. You want money that hit the til / bank during the month, even if the sale was invoiced earlier or later.

These two totals are meant to differ when customers pay later, pay deposits, or straddle weekends — that is normal.

3 · How to run this report (staff — numbered clicks)

Replace YOUR-SITE with your real address (examples: http://localhost/autowagen-master on a workshop PC or your live shop URL).

  1. Start Apache + database (your host handles this if you only use live hosting).
  2. Open the browser → go to login: YOUR-SITE/auth/login.php
  3. Sign in with a staff username that has permission to see Reports.
  4. On the black top menu, click Reports.
  5. Click Sales summary (period).
  6. Set From (start date) and To (end date) → click Apply.
  7. Review the coloured summary cards (final sales · drafts · payments) and scroll through Top customers and the invoice list.
  8. To send a snapshot to someone: click Print / PDF → in the printer list pick Save as PDF → save the file → email or WhatsApp send the PDF.

5 · Short Q&A for clients

Q: Is this VAT return ready?
A: It is a management summary off the POS data. Your tax practitioner may still want your full invoice file and receipts — this speeds up conversation, not replacing SARS filings.

Q: Why are “sales” and “payments” different this month?
A: Many shops invoice on day 28 and receive EFT on day 2 of the next month. Use the explanation in section 2 above.

Q: Where is the website turnover?
A: Use Reports → Web shop orders and your own bank recon for deposits from online buyers.
