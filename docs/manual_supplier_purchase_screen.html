On-screen blocks (A–F = before and including payments; G = stock lines)

Letters match the vertical order on the page. Give this sheet to shop staff as a quick reference.

A

Reference

What it is: Optional Invoice / bundle name (e.g. inv002) plus Notes (delivery, your own reminders).

Why it is there: So you can recognise this purchase later without opening every record.

When to use: When creating the purchase or whenever you need to update the reference text.

How to save: Click the red Save purchase button (block E) after editing.

B

Bill (ZAR) — what you owe for this buy

What it is: Supplier’s total for this batch: amount, bill date, optional due date.

Why it is there: Drives Accounts payable (owed). “Remaining” in block F comes from bill minus payments.

When to use: When you know the real invoice total (you can correct it later). This is not the sum of part selling prices — those are set per part in block G.

How to save: Save purchase (block E).

C

Supplier or private seller

What it is: Either Registered supplier (dropdown, e.g. HASSAN NIZAMIE) or Private individual (name, SA ID, phone).

Why it is there: Legal and business record of who you bought the batch from. One seller per purchase; many parts link to that purchase.

When to use: At setup and if the seller details change (rare).

How to save: Save purchase (block E).

D

ID / address on file

What it is: Uploads: ID or CIPC scan; proof of residence (with “I have this on file” where used).

Why it is there: Second-Hand Goods Act style compliance, especially for private sellers. Registered suppliers may not need files here.

When to use: When you receive scans from a private seller or when documents are updated.

How to save: Choose file(s) → Save purchase (block E).

E

Save purchase

What it is: Red button that saves blocks A + B + C + D together (the main purchase “header” and uploads).

Why it is there: Payments (F) are saved with the blue Add button — different action, so the buttons are separate.

When to use: After any change to reference, bill, supplier/private fields, or compliance files.

Do not use for: Recording money paid — use block F → Add.

F

Payments (ZAR)

What it is: Line showing Remaining / Bill / Paid; table of payment lines; form to add amount, date, method, bank ref, note → Add.

Why it is there: The bill says what you owe; each payment reduces what is still owed. Report: Inventory → Accounts payable (owed).

When to use: Each time you actually pay (EFT, cash, card) against this purchase. Part payments = several lines.

How to save: Complete the row → click blue Add (not Save purchase).

G

Parts in this purchase

What it is: List of stock lines (SKU, name, line type, asking price) tied to this batch; buttons to edit each part or add another.

Why it is there: One purchase, many parts. Seller/compliance stay on the purchase; each part holds what you sell it for and yard location (on the part screen).

When to use: When goods arrive — add lines; when prices or details change — Edit on a row.

How to save: Use Add part to this purchase / Edit — those screens save the part. Header changes still need Save purchase in E.

Quick rule for staff:

  • Changed bill, supplier, notes, or uploads? → Save purchase (E).
  • Paid money to the supplier for this purchase? → Add in Payments (F).
  • Added or fixed a part line? → Use the part add/edit flow (G).
