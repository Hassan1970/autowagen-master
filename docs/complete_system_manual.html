0 — Using this manual & screenshots

What you hold. This is the full written guide in one document. It is meant to match what Autowagen Master does today: vehicles you strip or stock, customers you sell to, suppliers you buy from, physical parts, batch purchases, money you owe suppliers, and shop-floor sales (POS).

Why screenshots matter. Text tells staff what to click; a picture shows where it is on their screen. This manual lists every recommended capture with a fixed filename so your folder stays organised.

How to capture (Windows). Open the live page in your training database. Press Win+Shift+S, drag around the area, paste into Paint, save as PNG into docs/manual_screenshots/. Then edit this HTML file and uncomment the matching <img> line under that figure.

Order of training. New users: read §1 → §2, then either follow §4–§7 (stock in) before §9 (sales), or jump to §9 if someone else already loaded vehicles/parts.

1 — Opening the app & signing in

1.1 Browser address

Autowagen Master runs in a normal web browser. On your workshop PC the address usually looks like:

http://localhost/autowagen-master/  or the hostname your installer gave you.

The first page either sends you to the login screen or to the dashboard if you are already signed in.

Figure 1. Browser address bar showing your Autowagen URL (homepage or redirect).

1.2 Login

  1. Open the login page (auth/login.php).
  2. Enter your username and password (your owner creates these in the database or an earlier setup step).
  3. Click Log in. Wrong passwords may be rate-limited after several attempts (security).

1.3 Logout

Click your name (top right) → Logout. Always logout on a shared PC.

2 — Dashboard & top menu

After login you see the Dashboard. The black top bar is the same on (almost) every page.

2.1 Menu map

  • Dashboard — home / overview.
  • EPC — electronic parts catalogue (categories), not your stock count.
  • Master data — Vehicles, Customers, Suppliers (people & companies you work with).
  • Inventory — All parts; links to open the public shop and stripping catalogue; Add part (staff+); Supplier purchases / New supplier purchase (staff+).
  • Reports — Accounts payable (owed) (staff+); Accounts receivable (owed); Sales invoices; Sales summary (period) (turnover vs payments-by-date · print/PDF); shortcut to Customer statements (opens Customers list — use Statement on a row); Web shop orders & Web shop messages (staff+).
  • POS — Sales invoices, New sale (staff+).

3 — EPC — parts catalogue tree

The EPC is a six-level tree (e.g. Engine → … → Variant). You use it to label where a part belongs in the catalogue. It does not replace inventory: a sellable part still needs a row under Inventory → All parts.

3.1 Browse tree (everyone)

  1. EPC → Browse tree.
  2. Click through levels until you reach variants.
  3. Use this when you need a standard label for documentation or linking.

Figure 6. EPC browse page with at least two levels visible.

3.2 Manage tree (owner / admin)

EPC → Manage tree adds/edits/deactivates catalogue nodes. Day-to-day yard staff rarely need this.

4 — Master data — vehicles

Each vehicle is a unit in your yard: donor car, stock number, stripping status, legal papers, photos, and links to stripped parts.

4.1 Vehicle list

  1. Master data → Vehicles.
  2. Use search/filters as needed. Columns typically include stock code, make/model, status, papers completeness, yard location.
  3. Add vehicle opens the editor without an id.

Figure 8. Vehicles admin list with at least one row.

docs/manual_screenshots/full-08-vehicles-admin.png

4.2 Vehicle editor (sections)

The edit screen is long; it is grouped into cards: stock & identity, status & specs, acquisition, legal papers (logbook, receipt, seller ID, proof of residence — as used in your build), photos, notes, yard, and linked EPC variants. After save, you can upload scans and photos.

Important: Stock code, VIN, and registration plate are uniqueness-checked — duplicates block save.

5 — Master data — customers

Customers are buyers (and contacts for on-account). For Second-Hand Goods Act-style sales, the system can require ID document and proof of address scans on the customer before certain POS lines can be finalized.

5.1 Customer list & modal

  1. Master data → Customers.
  2. Add or edit via the modal: individual vs business drives SA ID vs company reg fields.
  3. Upload compliance documents; list shows a Docs N/2 style badge when wired in your build.

6 — Master data — suppliers

Registered suppliers you buy from (as a business). Used when parts are sourced from a supplier rather than a once-off private seller (depending on how you use part source).

  1. Master data → Suppliers.
  2. Add contact name, phone, email, payment terms, etc.
  3. List and edit like other master tables.

7 — Inventory — parts (list & add/edit)

A part is sellable stock: SKU, name, source (stripped / OEM / replacement / third party), condition, status (on vehicle / available / reserved / sold / scrapped), qty, pricing, yard slot, photos, optional EPC links, and (for third party) compliance document fields or linkage to a supplier purchase.

7.1 All parts

  1. Inventory → All parts.
  2. Filter by source, status, vehicle, search SKU/name/yard.
  3. From a draft invoice you may land here with a blue Back to invoice bar and an Add to invoice column (only when opened from POS with a return link; see §9).

Figure 15. Parts inventory list with filters.

7.2 Add / edit part

  1. Inventory → Add part or pencil icon on a row.
  2. Choose source — the form shows different fields (vehicle link for stripped, supplier vs private for third party, etc.).
  3. SKU may be suggested; condition and status must reflect reality before you sell.
  4. Upload photos; link EPC variants if you use the catalogue.
  5. Save.

  1. Inventory → Supplier purchases — list existing batches.
  2. New supplier purchase — create header (seller/supplier, dates, uploads as per screen).
  3. Open a purchase → add or open linked parts.

Accounts receivable (short pointer)

For who owes you money after final invoices — use Reports → Accounts receivable (owed) (customer_ar_report.php). Printable statements: Statement link from that report, Reports → Customer statements (Customers list), or Master data → Customers.

Sales summary (period)

Reports → Sales summary (period) (sales_summary_report.php): filter by invoice date range · totals for final / draft / void in that range · payments summed by their paid date (often different from sales) · when credit-note tables exist (sql/07_credit_notes.sql), the same date inputs also drive a block of finalized credit notes filtered by each note’s credit date, split into AR reduction vs cash refund, plus a list · top customers · invoice list (up to 200 rows) · Print / PDF. Covers POS invoices only — web shop turnover stays under Web shop orders.

Customer credit notes (Stage 7): full workflow under Reports → Credit notes; detail balances also on AR and statements.

10 — Public web shop (guest buyer, no login)

Why it exists. Buyers browse New spare parts listed by staff (List on public website). There is no payment gateway in MVP: after checkout staff contact the buyer outside the software.

URL (adjust host/path). Local Laragon examples: catalogue http://localhost/autowagen-master/shop/ · cart /shop/cart.php · checkout /shop/checkout.php · thanks /shop/thanks.php.

  1. Open Spares shop (home lists parts that qualify — see eligibility below).
  2. Search by SKU/name or browse categories/menu (including gearbox submenu when configured).
  3. Click a part → part detail shows price (ex VAT on screen varies by layout), qty, photos if any → Add to cart.
  4. Cart icon → adjust qty → Checkout.
  5. Enter required Name & Phone; optional email, delivery/collection notes.
  6. Place order → Thanks page shows WEB-YYYY-NNNNN order number → buyer should keep it.
  7. If a part cannot be purchased online (used/stripped/disallowed condition etc.), guest may still use Enquire about this item where shown.

Eligibility rule (staff must set data correctly). Only parts matching shop rules appear as buy-online (SHGA-aligned): e.g. source not “Stripped”, condition New / Good / Fair for OEM new or Replacement as configured — Poor & Scrap are enquiry-only. Staff: see part edit → List on public website; list only when legal & business rules agree.

11 — Staff: list parts online & web shop orders

  1. Log into staff app → Inventory → All parts → edit a part (New + allowed source/type per policy).
  2. In Yard location & notes (or adjacent card): tick List on public website · Save.
  3. Open public shop: Inventory → Public shop (website) (opens /shop/).
  4. New orders → Reports → Web shop orders · find row WEB-… → Open for lines & totals.
  5. Placing guest order reduces stock same family as POS finalize. If cancelled: use Cancel order & restore stock where available — confirm popup.
  6. Staff still contact buyer for payment & collection (no gateway in MVP).

12 — Public stripping stock catalogue

Website section for wreck / stripping yard vehicles (not sold as cart line-items like SKU parts).

  1. Guests: /shop/stripping/ · filter/search · vehicles list with thumbnails from vehicle photos.
  2. Vehicle detail → Full gallery & specs (/shop/stripping/vehicle.php?id=…).
  3. Enquire about parts prefills enquiry form toward staff.
  4. Staff link (when logged in): Inventory → Stripping stock (website) (checks public URL).

13 — Guest enquiries inbox (staff)

If sql/06e_shop_guest_enquiries.sql ran on database: guests submit messages (/shop/enquiry.php).

  1. Open Reports → Web shop messages (guest enquiries) (shop_enquiries_admin.php; staff+).
  2. Read incoming messages linked to SKU/part context where saved.
  3. Reply by phone/email/WhatsApp — system stores inquiry; outbound email from server may be added later.

14 — Accounts receivable & customer statements

  1. Reports → Accounts receivable (owed): customer balances from final invoices minus payments · filters (e.g. overdue, as-at date).
  2. Print / PDF from AR report where offered.
  3. Customer statement: Statement from AR row or Customers list · printable · WhatsApp text helper / mailto for summary (attach PDF from browser print).
  4. On-account sales: after 06a_customer_account.sql, customers may be flagged Account customer; invoice finalize with due date expects that where configured.

16 — Roles & who can do what

Your installation defines users with a role (e.g. owner, admin, manager, staff, viewer). Only some roles may add parts, finalize sales, manage EPC, run backups, or see prices. The exact matrix follows how auth_check.php gates each page — if a menu item is missing, the login you used does not have that permission.
